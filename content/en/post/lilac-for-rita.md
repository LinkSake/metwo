<pre>
six-thirty, 
the sky splits—
pale yellow
where the sun falls, 
blue
above my head. 
The greenest park 
in the city,
tall pines 
that never lose their needles, 
a sycamore
that keeps its bark.
And this lilac, 
alone among so much
that stays,
leafless,
full of seed pods—
yellow-brown,
clustered at the crown
where I cannot reach them.

My grandmother's lilac 
was the center of her patio. 
After she died, 
someone said it brought bad luck. 

They cut it down.

I don't remember when I stopped
seeing lilacs. 
Until recently—
I picked a seed from the ground,
carried it in my pocket
like I did as a boy.

Now I stand 
beneath this tree
without leaves, 
in the greenest park,
looking up 
at what I cannot touch.
The seed pod still in my pocket—
small, 
dry, 
impossible to lose.
</pre>
