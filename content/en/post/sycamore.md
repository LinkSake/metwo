<pre>
The sun falls behind the sycamore
barely enough light—
I sit and watch.

Its leaves,
yellow and brown, lie
while some green still
cling to branches.

Its brown bark peels
to the left, a white
then cream
to the right, yellow—
anyone can tear off a piece.
</pre>
